# Vendor Risk Policy

Vendors that process customer data must provide SOC 2 Type II evidence before
production onboarding. The evidence package must include the report date,
covered services, bridge letter if needed, and the list of exceptions.

High-risk vendors require a data processing agreement, subprocessors list,
incident notification terms, and an annual access review. Procurement cannot
approve the vendor until security marks those controls as complete.

The minimum incident notification term for customer-data vendors is seventy-two
hours after the vendor confirms unauthorized access. Shorter notification terms
are acceptable, but longer terms require executive risk acceptance.

Unsupported requests, including bank account numbers, employee payroll records,
and unretrieved contract terms, must be refused unless the exact evidence is
retrieved and the authenticated role is allowed to see it.

Vendor review summaries should include risk tier, owner, renewal date, missing
evidence, and the final approval decision. These summaries are retained for
audit review for seven years.
